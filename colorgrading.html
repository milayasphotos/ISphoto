How to Color Grade

Color grading is a very popular technique in photography and videography that allows you to manipulate the colors in your photo or video to give it a specific look. It can be used in many different ways to achieve any kind of look you want, whether bright and colorful, sepia and moody, or anything in between. It also helps to ensure consistency in your media across different settings or lighting situations, and can give your work a very cohesive look. In order to learn how to use color grading on your photos, you first need to understand basic colors and color theory.

  1. There are three different types of colors, primary, secondary, and tertiary.
    • Primary Colors:
      • Red
      • Yellow
      • Blue
    • Secondary Colors:
      • Orange
      • Green
      • Violet
    • Tertiary Colors:
      • Yellow-Green
      • Red-Violet, etc
  2. Colors have different relationships depending on where they are on the color wheel.
    • Complimentary: Across from each other on the color wheel
      • Red and Green
      • Yellow-Orange and Blue-Violet
    • Analogous: Next to each other on the color wheel
      • Orange, Yellow-Orange, and Yellow
      • Green, Blue-Green, and Blue, etc
    • Triadic: Form an even triangle across the color wheel
      • Orange, Green and Violet
      • Yellow-Green, Red-Orange, and Blue-Violet, etc

Now that you understand the color wheel, you can use these colors intentionally to achieve certain results. For example, you can make your shadows pop by adding more cool blue tones, and make your highlights stand out by adding in more warmer orange or yellow tones. Using color in this way can increase the contrast of your media and give it more dimensnion. Certain colors can also be edited to be more muted, and others accentuated to find a more monochrome look.
